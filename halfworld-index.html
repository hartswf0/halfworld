Halfworld

Halftone + HALFTRACK + world. One paper substrate (ink dots on cream), one cast rig (pose channels), one score (cue rows over a scrubable clock), one mind contract (bounded op-packets), one memory (project JSON). Each artifact below is half a world; the engine is what they become when the seams are welded.

The rule of the engine: character ⊂ scene ⊂ world, and every layer is plain JSON that a hand, a game loop, a score, or an AI director can speak.

And the last seam is welded: the studio rasterizes its stage to BEFLIX-128 and shoots the score into an operator-beflix-scenes pack — so a composed HALFWORLD scene opens inside OPERATOR as an editable, branchable, publishable film. HALFWORLD is OPERATOR’s scene-camera; OPERATOR is HALFWORLD’s timeline, branch tree, and press.

More: the whole builder is hosted inside OPERATOR as F · STUDIO — spawn the full cast, place assets, pose and walk them, or hit CAPTURE to drive the selected character with your webcam. The shutter shoots the stage straight onto the film tree, and you can sit in calls as a character. On first launch, HERO, JUNE and BOSS break the fourth wall and give the tour themselves — walking the interface, pointing at every control, and performing each operation (they draw, add frames, fork, play, and take the shot) so the machine explains itself in-world. The UI is the story engine; the onboarding is the story.

And the two studios became one surface: the HALFWORLD stage runs chrome-less behind OPERATOR’s own controls (cast, pose, style, camera-drive, shoot), so F · STUDIO looks and works like the rest of the lab. SHOOT SCORE → SCENES turns the score’s beats into named scenes on the film tree — they group as scenes in DIRECT and run as batches in BRANCH. A scene name in AUTHOR tags every unit — Lens, Write, Prompt, Stage — so the whole film organizes itself by scene.

WHAT IT IS · THE DEMI-MONDE
HALFWORLD is a digital studio of “peaches with a flaw” — a poetic limbo of fragmented text and halftone ink where lonely characters, homeless scenes, and bodies without minds are welded together by shared contracts, sampling a beautiful abundance of outcasts into a single, publishable film. after Alexandre Dumas fils · le demi-monde

Dumas coined the demi-monde for a half-world of people who belonged neither to high society nor the gutter — a halfway house of the beautiful and the compromised. HALFWORLD is its technical translation. Every artifact indexed below is half of something: a cast with no world to stand in, a world with no one to inhabit it, a score with no stage, a mind with no body. They are the bruised, discarded fruit of nine separate programs — the peaches with a flaw. The engine does not try to make them whole in isolation. It says they taste just as good, and builds a cinematic medium out of their very fragmentation.

The half-world
Dumas’s social limbo becomes a technical limbo — a studio for gorgeous pieces that could not talk to the others until shared contracts welded them.
Peaches with a flaw
Characters without places, places without characters, scores without stages. Not repaired — sampled. The flaw is the grain of the film.
The double standard
In Dumas everyone is bound by one rigid contract. Here the AI mind and the human finger obey the exact same canonical verbs — so no matter how powerful the collaborator, the half-world stays safe, legible, intact. The law of the packet.
Ink & residue
Its citizens live in halftone dots on cream and commit to the film in 128×96 BEFLIX — the outcast rendered not as a failed photograph but as a woodcut. The medium is the fragment made grammar.

And the demi-monde knows it is one. On first arrival, the cast break the fourth wall and walk the interface to explain their own isolation — performing each control as they go, showing rather than telling. The onboarding is the story: outcasts giving you a tour of the halfway house they were welded into.

ORGAN 02 World what exists · assets, libraries, places
Two builders on one dot-field substrate. Worldsmith is the minimal open-vocabulary sketch; Atlantis is the maximal recursive studio. Neither can yet cast a Halftrack character.
Worldsmith worldsmith.html
"Start with nothing." Type any word and a deterministic dot-sprite appears — ~30 hand painters plus a seeded fallback (creature / totem / plant / machine) so the vocabulary is genuinely open. Ground-plane depth, groups, scene graph, freehand draw, voice notes, void/paper themes. Fully offline; nothing persists.
Gives the engine — the generative floor: "type to create anything is a promise, not a menu." The word→asset move every other tool should inherit.

Gives the engine — the STAGE + MEMORY contracts: asset / instance / world schema, recursion, the append-only operation ledger, and the atlantis-world-studio/v1 project snapshot that proves the whole state can round-trip as JSON.

ORGAN 03 Score & Mind when things happen · who decides
Two SYNTHCINE calls on one ICARO parametric face/body rig — identical bones, opposite clocks. Whispers is live and agentic; the Call is scored and scrubbed. The engine needs both at once.
Synthcine Whispers synthcine-whispers (5).html
"A room to think in." Five halftone heads on a call plus YOU on webcam. WHISPER perturbs a character's private interior monologue; affect ripples to neighbours through an adjacency graph and decays toward baseline. SPEAK gets an in-character reply. Ambient thoughts keep the room alive; every face runs emotion registers, tics, visemes, and a head-carriage spring.
Gives the engine — the agentic harness: interior vs. exterior speech, the affect-ripple propagation model, and PLAY AS (inhabiting a cast member with your body).
agentswhisper / speak affect ripplesllm-drivenlive, unscored
Synthcine Call — Body Rig SYNTHCINE_CALL_BODY_RIG_REBUILT (1).html
Scene 06 as live halftone puppetry with a real transport: 82.5 seconds, 24fps timecode, an OTIO line manifest as the score — every line {c, s, d, t, m, wpm} with motion cues compiled to rig effects and text compiled to visemes. Scrub-deterministic: everything derives from the playhead. Records webcam mocap takes onto the same timeline; exports BEFLIX macros.
Gives the engine — the SCORE contract: wall-clock cue rows (the same pattern as the MONTE transports), the cue grammar name@word, and takes as first-class timeline citizens.

ORGAN 04 Worldtext how it is navigated · how a mind edits a world
One planet you walk through by talking to it. The world answers in matter — and in a strictly bounded language of operations.
Virentis — Operational World Field virentis-field-icaro.html
A 31×31 tile field with fog-of-war: biomes, features, routes, relations, memories, weather. Turn-based — touch, move, or speak, and a world-mind (local rules or any LLM) replies with one utterance plus at most eight validated world-mutation ops (move, reveal, terrain, feature, route, rename, remember, relation, pulse). The whole world exports as dated JSON.
Gives the engine — the MIND contract: agents never touch pixels; they emit bounded, validated op-packets against the same schema a human's gestures use. Also the navigable worldtext the atlas becomes.
op-packet contractfog of war turn-basedjson exportmodel-agnostic

Façade — World Engine synthcine-facade-world-engine.html
"One continuous social room" — the same scene as a first-person walkable 3D apartment with a real-time Director, beat functions, a social-state sim (embarrassment, composure, suspicion, urgency, tension, affinity), interactable props, and endings. Fully local, fully hardcoded — the clearest picture of what playing inside the engine should feel like.
Gives the engine — embodied navigation and the social-meter vocabulary; also the cautionary tale: its literal geometry and JS beats are exactly what the STAGE and SCORE contracts must replace.

The call grid plus a live social-drama dock: a declarative beat graph (guards over world-facts → line sequences → timed reactions) and a phrase library with resource arbitration, so simultaneous performances never fight over the same body part. Player intents classified and answered in scene; endings earned from meters.
Gives the engine — the most authorable drama layer: BEATS and PHRASES are the shape agent-written scenes should take, one level above the SCORE.
